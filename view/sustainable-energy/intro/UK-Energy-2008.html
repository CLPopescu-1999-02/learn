Energy required to make stuff

One thing that the energy breakdown's above have not included is the energy required to make the stuff that we import. David MacKay has an interesting section on this in his book. He comes up with a rough figure of 48 kWh/d per person. He references several studies that have come to similar conclusions.

The energy used in industry and the energy embodied in imported stuff minus exports would equate to the energy that we use to make our stuff.

Energy in industry + imports = 84 kWh/d per person
Which compares to 82 kWh/d used in the domestic and service sectors as a whole.

David MacKay: Stuff, stuff 2

What is energy industry use of energy?

DECC Energy balance statistics methodology PDF

Energy industry use is consumption by both extraction and transformation industries to support the transformation process (but not for transformation itself). Typical examples are the consumption of electricity in power plants (eg for lighting, compressors and cooling systems) and the use of extracted gases on oil and gas platforms for compressors, pumps and other uses. The headings in this section are identical to those used in the transformation section with the exception of Pumped storage which reports on the electricity used to pump water to the reservoir.

Calculating kWh/d per person from tonnes of oil equivalent

Many of DECC's statistics are in millions or thousands of tonnes of oil equivalent. I have converted these quantities into kWh/d/per person as I find I can relate to this unit better. If your interested in doing the same maybe to check up on my figures, this is how I did it:
 
Lets convert the 2008 total primary energy value, table 1.1, cell B45 from DECC's overall energy spreadsheet.
 
Total primary energy consumption 2008 = 225.4 million tonnes of oil equivalent
 
1) Lets start by creating a conversion factor to convert 1 million tonnes of oil equivalent/year into kWh/d/per person (in the UK)
 
1 ton of oil equivalent = 11,630 kWh
http://www.decc.gov.uk/en/content/cms/statistics/source/notes/notes.aspx
 
1 million tonnes of oil equivalent = 11630000000 kWh
 
There are  61,858,000 people in the UK (DECC domestic, table 3.3)
 
We can therefore calculate kWh per person consumed in the UK for every million tonnes of oil equivalent consumed:
 
kWh per person = 11630000000 kWh /  61,383,000 = 189.47kWh per person per million tonnes of oil
 
To find kWh per day divide this figure by 365: 189.47kWh/year / 365 = 0.52 kWh/d per person per million tonnes of oil
 
2) And so 225.4 million tonnes of oil equivalent is equivalent too: 225.4 x 0.52 = 117 kWh/d per person (in the UK)
 
Important factors
Million tonnes of oil equivalent to kWh/d/per person (UK) 0.52
Thousand tonnes of oil equivalent to kWh/d/per person (UK) 0.00052
